Terminologies in Environmental Biology:

  • Biosphere:


    The life sustaining envelope of Earth is called Biosphere.
    e.g. the surface of Earth on which life exists.

  • Biome:


    A bio-geographical region that is recognized by specific vegetation or climate is called Biome.
    e.g. different sub-levels on which life exists.

  • Biomass:


    Total mass of dry organic matter per unit area is called Biomass.

  • Population:


    A group of individuals that belong to same species living in a specific habitat (An area where living organisms live and maintain their lives is called as Habitat.) is called Population.
    e.g. parrots living on a same tree is their population.

  • Community:


    A group of different organisms or population living in a specific habitat is called Community.
    e.g. different birds of different species living in trees.

  • Ecosystem:


    The interaction of living organisms with non-living factors and energy is transferred from one level to another is called Ecosystem.
    e.g. our surroundings, interaction of plants & animals with water, air, soil and light, etc-.

  • Pyramid of Biomass:


    The total biomass of organisms at each tropic level is called Pyramid of Biomass. It shows that the each higher feeding level contains less biomassthan the previous tropic level. it results from energy loss in a food chain at each tropic level.

  • Food Chain:


    The transfere of food material and food energy from producers through a series of organisms i.e. producers to consumers with repeated eating and being eaten by other organisms and a chain is formed is called a Food Chain.
    e.g. plants --> grasshopper --> sparrow --> owl --> and so on...

  • Food Web


    When various food chains are linked together interect with eachother to form a complete network is called Food Web.
    e.g. grain to grass...{forms a food chain}, grain to mouse...{forms another food chain}
